Oral examination
The oral examination is individual, approximately 30 minutes. You will be expected to explain your system, draw diagrams, and answer questions demonstrating understanding of the concepts — not just the implementation.

1. Presentation (10 min) — present your system with a live demo.
2. Whiteboard session (20 min) — draw and explain diagrams on the whiteboard.

You may not use slides or notes during the whiteboard session.

What You Must Be Able To Do
Draw and Explain System Architecture
The architecture of your system and the responsibilities of its major components.
How sensors, services, data storage, decision-making components, and actuators interact.
Why particular communication patterns, technologies, and deployment choices were selected.
Autonomous System Design
How your system senses, reasons, and acts on its environment.
How decisions are made and translated into actions.
The role of autonomy in your system and the limits of that autonomy.
How the system behaves under uncertainty, missing information, or incorrect decisions.
Data and Decision-Making
How data flows through the system from sensing to action.
How data is stored, processed, and transformed into information used for decision-making.
How autonomous decisions are generated, validated, and executed.
Explain Your Testing Strategy
How the system was tested and evaluated.
How failures, incorrect sensor readings, communication problems, or component outages affect system behaviour.
The limitations of the current design and possible improvements.
Example questions
Be prepared to answer questions such as:

What makes a system autonomous rather than automated?
How does your system implement a sense–reason–act loop?
What happens if a sensor provides incorrect information?
What happens if a decision-making component becomes unavailable?
Why was a particular communication pattern chosen?
How does your system balance local and cloud-based computation?
What are the main failure modes of your system?
How would your architecture change if the system were deployed at a larger scale?
Tips
Practice drawing your architecture from memory before the exam.
Be honest about what you don't know — it's better than guessing.
If you used AI tools to generate code, you must still understand what the code does.
Focus on why, not just what — the examiner wants to see reasoning, not memorisation.
